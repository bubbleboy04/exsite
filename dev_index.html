  • Football QB Sim was the first completed solo project released
  • Project began with a basic ball throwing mechanism that translated a ball object in a straight line from an elevated position
  • Tested colliders on the ball and on a receiver standin to see how Unity would handle the catching of the ball
  • Created very basic receiver models with in-engine GameObjects for further testing.
  • Precoded routes by using coroutines in script that uses percentage of remaining field and receiver rotation
  • Began adding more set decoration to make the environment more visually appealing
  • In an effort to make the throwing more realistic, I started looking into new methods to handle the ball throw.
  • Had to find a solution that would result in short throws having a low arc and deeper throws having a higher arc.
  • Once fixed velocity throwing methods had been exhausted, I began looking into different physics concepts for the desired result.
  • Angle of Reach formula was found and applied with a fixed velocity, which resulted in higher arcs and more realistic throws.
  • With an accurate throwing mechanic, the controlled QB could receive a character model and be placed on the field.
