    • Football Quarterback Simulator

  • First completed solo project
  • Uses Unity's physics RayCast systems and RigidBody systems to create a robust ball throwing mechanic
  • Further features include color customization, receiver route selection, and proper difficulty scaling
  • Futher plans to make features more robust with a future remake of this game
  • Project began with an extremely basic and simple method to throw the ball in the form of moving the ball in a straight line originating from the player GameObject
  • Early versions also included defense AI that had precoded paths and timers to know when to begin chasing the receivers
  • In an attempt to make the ball travel in a more convincing arc path, RigidBodies in Unity were implemented in order to add forces to the ball
  • Angle of Reach formula was used to calculate the angle the ball should travel at to reach an appropriate distance given a fixed velocity
  • Defense AI was retweaked later in development, removing the precoded timers and using a function that would return true if the receiver began moving away from the defender
  • With a randomly generated delay, the new defense AI is much more reactive and allows for a wider variety of route implementations
